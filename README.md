# Business Analysis Case Study
### When My Father Became My Client – Applying Business Analysis at Home

## About This Project
This is a personal Business Analysis case study where I applied core BA 
competencies to solve a real-life problem at home. After my father retired 
from the banking sector, he struggled to adapt to digital platforms like 
HRMS portals, digital KYC, and app-based medical benefits.

I approached this as a Business Analyst — using structured frameworks to 
understand his challenges, define requirements, and design a solution to 
simplify his digital transition.

## Problem Statement
A retired senior citizen facing difficulty transitioning from decades of 
manual processes to modern digital workflows — password resets, KYC 
renewals, document management, and medical benefit apps.

## BA Frameworks & Tools Applied
- Stakeholder Analysis & Engagement
- Current-State (As-Is) & Future-State (To-Be) Assessment
- Gap Analysis
- Business Requirements Document (BRD)
- MoSCoW Prioritization
- RACI Matrix
- Use Case Diagrams
- Agile Epics, User Stories & Acceptance Criteria
- Solution Design & Wireframing
- Fit-Gap Analysis & Risk Mitigation
- Change Management & UAT

## Project Highlights
- Mapped the full As-Is process and identified key pain points
- Documented functional, non-functional and transition requirements
- Designed wireframes for a mobile dashboard with document upload, 
  reminders, and help & support features
- Wrote Agile User Stories with Given-When-Then Acceptance Criteria
- Created a post-implementation support and sustainability plan

## Key Outcome
Successfully designed a simplified, user-friendly solution that empowered 
the user to manage post-retirement digital tasks independently — reducing 
confusion, errors, and dependency on external help.

## Skills Demonstrated
Business Analysis | Requirements Elicitation | Stakeholder Management |
Process Mapping | Agile Methodology | Documentation | Empathy-Driven Design
